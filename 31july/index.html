 SIA-India submission on TRAI Consultation Paper on the Framework for Granting Services Authorization

The Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) released a consultation paper on the Framework for Service Authorizations to be granted under the Telecommunications Act, 2023 on July 11, 2024. TRAI's proactive approach to drafting a framework for service authorizations under the new Telecommunications Act is commendable. In response, SIA-India has submitted comprehensive recommendations after extensive consultation with its members, advocating for replacing licenses with authorizations to expedite processing, ensuring clear and concise authorizations with essential details, and adopting a flexible regulatory framework to allow for dynamic adjustments. Additionally, SIA-India suggests exploring unified authorizations for similar services, implementing safeguards to protect service providers, and addressing specific concerns of the satellite communication industry, including exemptions from the USO levy and reductions in the SUC.

 DoT Unveils New Reimbursement Scheme to Support Startups and MSEs in Telecom Industry

The Department of Telecommunications (DoT) has introduced a new reimbursement scheme aimed at easing financial pressures on startups and Micro & Small Enterprises (MSEs) in the telecom sector. This initiative, announced at the India Mobile Congress 2024, seeks to promote domestic manufacturing, attract investments, and boost exports.

Under the scheme, startups and MSEs can receive up to INR 50 lakhs to cover testing and certification costs necessary for product quality and market access. The reimbursement rates are as follows:
    • Startups: 75% reimbursement
    • Micro-enterprises: 60% reimbursement
    • Small enterprises: 50% reimbursement

With a total fund allocation of Rs. 25 crore, this initiative underscores DoT's dedication to fostering equitable growth and innovation within the telecom industry.

For more information on eligibility criteria and application guidelines, visit the DoT official website: https://ttdf.usof.gov.in/reimbursement

 ISRO Successfully Demonstrates Air Breathing Propulsion Technology

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) achieved a milestone with the successful second experimental flight of its Air Breathing Propulsion Technology today at 07:00 am. Launched from the Satish Dhawan Space Centre, Sriharikota, the RH-560 Sounding rocket, equipped with propulsion systems on either side, demonstrated satisfactory performance. During the flight, approximately 110 parameters were monitored, and the data collected will aid in the next development phase. Prior ground tests were conducted at ISRO Centres and CSIR-NAL, Bengaluru. The RH-560, a cost-effective test bed, is ISRO’s heaviest-sounding rocket, advancing technology with each mission.

NASA Seeks Input for LunaRecycle Initiative to Tackle Waste Management for Lunar Missions

NASA has unveiled the LunaRecycle initiative, a part of its Centennial Challenges Program, aimed at developing sustainable waste management solutions for long-term lunar missions. With the Artemis Program's crewed launches on the horizon, the initiative seeks to incentivize the design and development of recycling systems suitable for use on the moon’s surface and within pressurized lunar habitats. This initiative addresses the critical need to manage various waste streams, including solid waste, by minimizing, storing, processing, and recycling waste in a space environment. The goal is to ensure that little to no waste will need to be returned to Earth.

 Elena Geo Systems Pvt. Ltd Receives Telecom Excellence Award for NavIC Innovations

SIA-India commends Elena Geo Systems Pvt Ltd, as they have been awarded the prestigious Pandit Deendayal Upadhyaya Telecom Excellence Award-2023 for their NavIC innovations. This award underscores their commitment to cutting-edge technology and national security, ensuring India is equipped with vital technology for future challenges.

 Dhruva Space and Avantel Limited Secure IN-SPACe Authorization for GSaaS

Dhruva Space and Avantel Limited, two of the SIA-India esteemed members have received authorization from the Indian National Space Promotion and Authorization Center (IN-SPACe) for Ground Segment as a Service (GSaaS). This move will lead to a cost-effective, shared Ground Station infrastructure, reducing financial barriers for satellite operators. GSaaS in equatorial regions will offer extended satellite visibility, reduced latency, and improved signal reliability, optimizing global satellite operations. SIA-India congratulates them for achieving a milestone.
