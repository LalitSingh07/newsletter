 The successful culmination of the PRISM 1.0: “US – India Export Control Dialogue”

The inaugural session of the PRISM (Perspectives of Regulatory Issues in Strategic Markets) 2024 Webinar Series – “US-India Export Control Dialogue” successfully concluded, focusing on the complexities of export controls and their implications for U.S.-India collaboration in critical sectors like space systems and dual-use technologies. The series is jointly organized by SIA-India and IndUS Tech Council. The webinar explored pivotal topics, including ITAR, SCOMET, and EAR regulations, while emphasizing the importance of innovation and co-production.

The first webinar 1.0 comes at a crucial time ahead of critical reforms like ITAR (International Traffic in Arms Regulations), MTCR (Missile Technology Control Regime) liberalisation. Following recent amendments by the U.S. Department of Commerce aimed at easing restrictions on space-related items, which reclassifies many space-related products, including commercial satellites, from the U.S. Munitions List to the Commerce Control List, opens up new opportunities for U.S.-India cooperation in space. The session underscored the importance of clear regulatory frameworks and strong compliance measures.

We will be releasing a comprehensive report on the deliberations of the webinar shortly. Stay tuned for further updates as we are excited to announce that our upcoming webinars from the PRISM series will focus on other key strategic markets, including Australia and Japan. These sessions will culminate in a multi-national focused event India Space Congress scheduled from 25th – 27th June 2025 at Hotel Le Meridien, New Delhi (www.indiaspacecongress.com).

 Karnataka Unveils Draft Space Technology Policy to Position as Global Space Hub

The Karnataka government has introduced its Draft Space Technology Policy 2024-2029, aiming to capture 50% of India's space market and strengthen its national and global presence. Key initiatives include skill development programs to prepare professionals for space industry careers, investment incentives to attract both domestic and foreign investments, and the creation of dedicated manufacturing parks and testing centers to support space companies. The policy also focuses on fostering research, intellectual property, and marketing for startups and MSMEs, while promoting the adoption of space technologies in governance and industry. These efforts are set to position Karnataka as a leading global hub for space technology.

 Technology and Innovation
 

GSAT-20 Successfully Launched, Heralding a New Era of Satellite Connectivity

India’s most advanced communication satellite, GSAT-20 (GSAT-N2), was successfully placed into Geosynchronous Transfer Orbit (GTO) by SpaceX’s Falcon 9 rocket. This milestone marks a significant leap in satellite connectivity capabilities, aligning with initiatives like BharatNet, which aims to connect 6,55,968 villages across the country. Weighing 4,700 kg, GSAT-20 features a high-throughput Ka-band payload, capable of delivering a remarkable 48 Gbps data transmission capacity. The satellite is poised to revolutionize broadband services in India, especially in remote and underserved areas, while also enabling in-flight internet connectivity. As India continues to bridge its digital divide, GSAT-20 represents a crucial step in transforming connectivity for millions across the nation

 SIA-India Exhibiting the Indian Space Industry through “India Pavilion” at IPSEC

SIA-India, as a Knowledge Partner at the upcoming Indo-Pacific Space and Earth Conference and Exhibition (IPESEC), is set to lead the India Pavilion, highlighting the advancements and innovations of the Indian space sector.

 SIA-India advocated for AI Governance at the International Cyberlaw Conference 2024

On November 14, 2024, SIA-India’s Director General, Mr. Anil Prakash, participated in a panel discussion on "Artificial Intelligence Governance and Regulations, Frameworks, and Policies" at the International Conference on Cyberlaw, Cybercrime, and Cybersecurity. Addressing the transformative potential of AI in India’s space industry, Mr. Prakash emphasized its role in revolutionizing data-driven operations and expanding the Earth data economy. SIA-India Engages in a Roundtable Discussion with Ms. Thea D. Rozman, Assistant Secretary, BIS– US Department of Commerce

SIA-India recently represented the Indian space industry at a Roundtable meeting with Ms. Thea D. During the discussions, SIA-India Director General Mr. Anil Prakash emphasized the importance of examining US and Indian export control laws, particularly concerning dual-use technology regulations for companies in both nations.  SIA-India Welcomes Solar Industries India Limited as Corporate Member

SIA-India is delighted to announce Solar Industries India Limited as its newest Corporate Member. With a legacy of excellence in space technology applications, Solar Industries has made remarkable contributions to India’s space and defense sectors, including:
  1. Successfully conducting static tests of the PSOM-XL motor for ISRO's PSLV launch vehicle.
  2. Advancing indigenous defense capabilities with successful trials of the Nagastra loitering munition system, competing with global OEMs.
  3. Enhancing reliability in critical components like boosters and motors, bolstering India’s self-reliance in space technology.
  4. Innovating in advanced systems like chaffs and flares, showcasing a commitment to cutting-edge technologies.

Through a diverse team and collaborative partnerships, Solar Industries continues to drive innovation, reinforcing its role in strengthening India’s space capabilities. SIA-India warmly welcomes them to the community.
 

GalaxEye Space Advances Earth Observation with Tech Demo Aboard ISRO's PSLV POEM and $10 Million Series a Funding

SIA-India congratulates its member, GalaxEye Space, as they continues to push the boundaries of space innovation. GalaxEye is set to showcase its cutting-edge technology with the Mission Patch tech demo aboard ISRO's PSLV POEM module.
