India Space Congress 2024

 Burgeoning Success at India Space Congress 2024!  

This year, ISC took place from 26-28 June 2024 in New Delhi centered around an overarching theme of “Space Innovation: Bridging Boundaries, Transforming Tomorrow,” which serves as a compass guiding us through a cosmic odyssey of ideas and opportunities. This premier event beaconed as a catalyst for shaping the future of space technology, fostering collaboration, inspiring disruptive business models, driving investments, nurturing startups, and influencing regulatory and policy support.

The three-day event successfully brought together distinguished guests including the Chairman of ISRO, TRAI, and IN-SPACe for the first time emphasising the pivotal role of the conference in shaping the space sector. The event was also graced by Ambassadors from Australia, Indonesia, and Switzerland, Secretaries, the head of the Italian Space Agency, senior scientists, and officers from ISRO and DOT. The event further saw 850 delegates, 215 unique speakers from 36 nationalities and more than 300 organizations bringing together key stakeholders and luminaries from the space sector to chart a transformative path for India's space endeavours.

 Bridging Boundaries – Global Collaboration  

This year, at SIA-India, unveiled strategic global partnerships aimed at advancing "Viksit Bharat 2047," enhancing India’s space capabilities through international collaboration under the theme "Bridging Boundaries and Transforming Tomorrow." With the private sector as our co-travelers, India hosts over 400 firms and SMEs in the space industry, including 204 startups that have emerged predominantly post-2020, attracting a cumulative funding of $380 million to date. ISC 2024 marked a historic moment by bringing together Hon’ble Shri S. Somanath, Secretary DoS and Chairman of ISRO; Dr Pawan Kumar Goenka, Chairman of IN-SPACe; and Shri Anil Kumar Lahoti, Chairman of TRAI, on the same stage. The conference orchestrated a cosmic odyssey of deliberations involving global participation from 36 nations including our esteemed partners from Switzerland, Australia, US, Indonesia, and Italy. Additionally, the conference witnessed the release of 5 reports and whitepapers, alongside significant MoU signings that stands as a testament to the thriving demand for collaboration with Indian startups in the global market.

 MoUs

In a remarkable effort ISC 2024 witnessed five significant partnerships, solidifying through the exchange of Memorandum of Understanding (MoUs). These agreements highlight collaborative efforts aimed at advancing India's space capabilities and fostering international cooperation.
  • Mount Tech Growth Fund- Kavachh (MGF-Kavachh) and SIA-India: SIA-India signed an MoU with A Category II AIF, MGF-Kavachh, to support start-ups in the space ecosystem, focusing on innovation and deep technology. This collaboration aims to advance India’s "Atmanirbhar" vision, addressing strategic needs in Security, Defence & Aerospace, and Communications.
  • IndUS Tech and SIA-India: SIA-India exchanged an MoU with Indus Tech Council to bolster U.S.-India space technology innovation. This partnership aims to foster collaborative efforts among public and private sectors, and academia, nurturing the next generation of technology leaders in both nations.
  • Economic Council of India (ECI) and SIA-India: The MoU between ECI and SIA-India underscores a commitment to economic development through space technology initiatives, fostering opportunities for innovation and growth.
  • Elena Geo System Pvt Ltd and Pacific Satelit Nuastara,Indonesia
  • NSIL and Space Machine Company

The Indian Space industry saw an unprecedent Milestone with the Australian Government signing an $18 million MoU with ISRO's commercial arm, NewSpace India Limited (NSIL), embarking into a New Era for Indian Space Industry, at the India Space Congress 2024 !

 Exhibition  

The ISC 2024 exhibitions showcased leading companies and institutions across satellite manufacturing, launch services, ground segment solutions, and space applications. Highlights included expertise in defence space, advanced geospatial technologies, satellite systems, satellite imagery, communication solutions, space electronics innovations, geospatial data processing, high-performance computing, and research in space technology. The exhibitions at ISC 2024 served as a hub for networking, knowledge exchange, and showcasing the transformative potential of space technology for the benefit of humanity.

Call For Papers

India Space Congress is more than just a gathering; it embodies a celebration of innovation, collaboration, and the relentless pursuit of excellence in the space industry. As part of this celebration, ISC as per the pre-event initiatives organised ‘the Call for Papers initiative’, inviting researchers, professionals, and students from around the globe to present their groundbreaking ideas and research. The response was overwhelming, with submissions of exceptional quality.

Re-Orbit Joins as Member

SIA-India is thrilled to extend a warm welcome to our esteemed new member, ReOrbit! ReOrbit is a pioneering force in the new space era, specializing in in-space data delivery. Their advanced small satellite platform features autonomous operation and inter-networking capabilities, driven by a software-first approach for agile and timely space missions in any orbit. They provide reliable, cost-effective solutions with flight-proven subsystems and continuous software reconfiguration in orbit, enhancing satellite value over time. Partnering with leaders in security, satellite communication, and Earth observation, ReOrbit's technology is validated through collaborations with the European Space Agency, advancing global information accessibility through satellite innovation.
