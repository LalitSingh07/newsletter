Atmanirbhar Bharat in Action: Industry's Role in Strengthening National Security 

In the wake of India’s robust response to the Pahalgam terror attack, Operation Sindoor has underscored not just military precision but also the vital role of India’s homegrown defence industry. The swift neutralization of cross-border threats reflects the nation’s growing defence preparedness, enabled in no small part by industry leaders like Ananth Technologies and Solar Industries India Limited, both esteemed SIA-India members.  

SIA-India members, such as Ananth Technologies and Solar Industries India Limited, have stood out for their significant contributions during this critical time. Ananth Technologies, already recognized for its pioneering work in satellite systems and aerospace electronics, has further cemented its reputation by supporting advanced defence technologies that were integral to recent operations. Solar Industries, a leader in defence consumables, ensured uninterrupted supply of critical munitions, underlining the importance of a robust domestic defence manufacturing ecosystem.  

As the spotlight shines on Atmanirbhar Bharat, these contributions exemplify how Indian industry is not only rising to the occasion but also shaping the very architecture of national security. SIA-India salutes its members for their enduring commitment to safeguarding India’s sovereign interests through innovation, reliability, and unwavering support. 

 Policy and Regulatory

TRAI sets Satcom spectrum fee at 4% of revenues, extra ₹500 for urban users 

In a landmark move, the Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) has recommended a uniform spectrum usage charge (SUC) of 4% of the Adjusted Gross Revenue (AGR) for satellite communication (satcom) services. Additionally, urban-area customers may face a surcharge of ₹500 per terminal annually. Aimed at ensuring equitable spectrum pricing and promoting wider adoption, this proposal marks a significant shift from the current license-based model, paving the way for more efficient spectrum allocation in India's evolving satcom sector. 

"SIA-India welcomes TRAI’s progressive recommendations on satellite-based spectrum assignment as a pivotal step towards building an inclusive and future-ready digital infrastructure. The administrative allocation model, performance-linked AGR-based spectrum fee, and targeted exemptions for rural and remote users reflect a bold policy direction that supports affordability, scalability, and equitable access. By enabling flexible use of key satellite bands, aligning with DOT and IN-SPACe guidelines, and safeguarding national security through domestic gateway mandates, these recommendations lay the groundwork for accelerated satellite broadband deployment. This will be instrumental in bridging the digital divide, connecting underserved regions, and realizing the vision of Digital India and BharatNet." 

— Anil Prakash, DG, SIA-India

 Technology and Innovation

ISRO to launch All-Weather Radar Satellite RISAT-1B on May 18 

ISRO is set to launch the radar imaging satellite EOS-09 (RISAT-1B) aboard PSLV-C61 on May 18 from Sriharikota. Designed for high-resolution, all-weather, day-and-night Earth observation, the satellite will significantly enhance applications in agriculture, forestry, urban planning, disaster management, and national security. Equipped with Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) technology, RISAT-1B will provide vital data for strategic and civilian use, especially in the backdrop of the geopolitical sensitivities and underscoring India’s growing capabilities in advanced remote sensing and space-based surveillance systems. 

Key Specifications 

  • Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) 
  • 5-metre resolution 
  • 2-metre swath 
  • X-band radar 
  • Day-night, all-weather imaging 
  • High revisit capability

In Memoriam: Pradman Kaul (1940s – 2025)

We are deeply saddened to share the passing of Mr Pradman Kaul, former President and CEO of Hughes and an iconic figure in the global satellite communications industry. With a career spanning four decades, Kaul was instrumental in shaping global broadband satellite services and industry standards. Honoured by IEEE, the National Academy of Engineering, and the SSPI Hall of Fame, his legacy is one of innovation, leadership, and service. The global tech and satellite community mourns a transformative figure whose impact endures.

SIA-India at GLEX 2025 India 

The IAF Global Conference on Space Exploration (GLEX 2025) was held from 7–9 May 2025 in New Delhi, India. The conference served as a vital platform for sharing programmatic, technical, and policy insights among global space stakeholders, reinforcing international cooperation and knowledge exchange among nations committed to advancing humanity’s reach beyond Earth. 

SIA-India had the honour of participating in GLEX 2025, reaffirming its shared vision with ISRO for advancing global space exploration. Here are some glimpses from the event. 

1. At GLEX 2025, SIA-India strengthened global ties through a strategic meeting with Mr. Clay Mowry, President of the International Astronautical Federation (IAF), discussing pathways to enhance international cooperation and promote the commercial space agenda. The delegation also engaged with industry leaders, including Vast, and explored innovations showcased at the ISRO exhibition. These interactions reflect SIA-India’s ongoing commitment to shaping a globally integrated, future-ready space ecosystem driven by collaboration and innovation. 

2. SIA-India leadership participated in the Gujarat Space Policy Roundtable an initiative of the Gujrat Govt. during GLEX 2025. Contributing industry insights to strong regional space ecosystem. 

3. At GLEX 2025, Ms. Lisa Vitaris, Director of IAC 2025 Sydney, underscored the growing India-Australia space partnership. Building on a shared MoU, SIA-India and SIAA have co-hosted a pivotal webinar on “Unlocking Trade Potential in India-Australia Space Collaboration” and will soon release a joint report outlining key recommendations, including harmonising export controls, enabling joint R&D, co-incubation, and cross-border investments. SIA-India also held a meeting with SIAA Chairman Jeremy Hallett and Ms. Vitaris to align efforts between India Space Congress 2025 and IAC 2025.  

SIA-India Engages with ANRF CEO to Integrate Space Sector into National Research Agenda 

A delegation from SIA-India—comprising Mr. Pawan Kapur (Board Member), Mr. Anil Prakash (Director General), Mr. Rajeev Gambhir (Deputy Director General), and Mr. K. Anbalagan (Deputy Director, International Cooperation)—held a strategic meeting with Dr. Shivkumar Kalyanaraman, CEO of the Anusandhan National Research Foundation (ANRF). InspeCity Secures $5.6M Seed Round to Advance In-Orbit Servicing Capabilities 

InspeCity has raised $5.6 million in a seed round to propel development of India’s first vertically integrated platform for in-orbit satellite servicing and life extension. With core expertise in propulsion, robotics, and RPOD (Rendezvous, Proximity Operations, and Docking), the startup is redefining satellite sustainability. Backed since 2023 and validated by iDEX and global partnerships, InspeCity will now focus on space-qualifying its systems and scaling global deployments—strengthening India’s role in building a resilient space economy. 

Logic Fruit Technologies Advances Defence Innovation Under TDF Scheme 

Logic Fruit Technologies, a valued member of SIA-India, has achieved a key milestone under the DRDO’s Technology Development Fund (TDF) Scheme. The company’s RINC 818 Video Processing & Switching Module (AVPSM) delivers real-time, high-speed video processing with advanced multi-source integration. Designed for mission-critical ISR (Intelligence, Surveillance & Reconnaissance) operations, the AVPSM stands as a significant leap in indigenous defence technology, reinforcing India's capabilities in next-generation battlefield systems and situational awareness.
