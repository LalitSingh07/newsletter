“True Brahmastra- Brahmos” achieves 83% indigenisation and wants to offer unique precision

In a significant leap towards self-reliance in defense technology, BrahMos Aerospace, the Indo-Russian joint venture, has announced that its supersonic cruise missile system has achieved an 83% indigenization level. This milestone, as stated by BrahMos CEO & MD, Mr. A. D. Rane, is attributed to a "systematic approach and a renewed push for self-reliance." While the 83% indigenization level is a commendable achievement, it is expected that BrahMos will continue its efforts to achieve 100% indigenization by 2026. The company's ongoing focus on research and development, coupled with advancements in domestic technology and production capabilities, will be pivotal in achieving this goal.

SIA-India represents space sector’s interest in IN-SPACe Industry Meet

We are delighted to share that Mr Anil Prakash, Director General of the Satcom Industry Association (SIA-India ), represented the space industry's interests at the prestigious 'IN-SPACe Industry Meet' held on June 6, 2024, at Wyndham Ahmedabad Shela Imperia.

In his address, Mr Anil Prakash outlined a strategic vision for India's space sector: “INSPACe should set the role, responsibility, and accountability of the space association as a partner in realising India's vision for the space sector, aiming for a $44 billion contribution to the global economy. It must facilitate non-banking financial institutions' involvement in funding space initiatives, integrate MSMEs for sector growth, act as an arbitrator in government and PSU contracts, and implement punitive measures to remove growth barriers. Additionally, it should streamline the transfer of technology from ISRO/DRDO to industry stakeholders.”

This vision highlights the need for collaboration, robust funding mechanisms, and the smooth transfer of cutting-edge technology to drive growth and innovation in India's space sector.

Dhruva space’s Thybolt-1 and Thybolt-2 satellites have successfully concluded their mission after completing more than 15,000 orbits combined

Inmarsat Launches New Global Maritime Connectivity Service NexusWave

Inmarsat Maritime, a Viasat company, has introduced NexusWave, a comprehensive connectivity service for the maritime industry. NexusWave combines Global Xpress (GX) Ka-band, low-Earth orbit (LEO) services, coastal LTE, and an additional L-band layer for enhanced resilience, ensuring fast, unlimited, and always-on global internet coverage. Scheduled to integrate the high-speed ViaSat-3 Ka-band service by 2025, NexusWave aims to meet the growing demand for data consumption and secure, efficient global communication. This service transforms vessels into floating offices and homes, supporting digitalisation and crew welfare initiatives. Inmarsat's fully managed service guarantees seamless performance, backed by continuous enhancements and technical assistance, providing maritime operators with reliable, simplified, and secure connectivity solutions.

Viasat Energy Services Introduces Enhanced Hybrid Network Service for the Energy Industry

Viasat, Inc. has announced the launch of a new managed communications service specifically designed for remote oil and gas operations through its Energy Services division. This advanced service integrates low Earth orbit (LEO) satellite capacity with Viasat's existing multi-orbit and terrestrial networks, offering a seamless, flexible, and fully managed solution. Set to be available in the third quarter of 2024, the service will enhance operational efficiency, safety, and crew welfare for energy sector customers. It supports cloud-based computing, operational applications, and incorporates Viasat's cybersecurity and AI-driven Intelie™ platform.
