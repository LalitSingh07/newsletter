SIA-India Advocates for Protection of Satellite Spectrum in Response to TRAI Consultation

SIA-India has strongly advocated for the exclusive allocation of the 27.5–28.5 GHz frequency band for satellite services, in line with global best practices, to ensure satellite communications can provide last-mile connectivity, particularly in remote and underserved areas. The association also recommends utilizing the 27.0–31.0 GHz (uplink) and 17.7–21.2 GHz (downlink) bands for Fixed Satellite Services (FSS), including Non-Geostationary Satellite Orbit (NGSO) systems, to boost satellite capabilities in India.

To promote long-term investments in satellite infrastructure, SIA-India has proposed spectrum assignments with a 20-year tenure and pricing set at 1% of Adjusted Gross Revenue (AGR) for NGSO-based services. The association stresses the importance of secure and predictable access to spectrum to drive innovation and ensure India’s satellite communications can meet growing demands across commercial, civil, and military sectors while continuing to bridge the digital divide across the country.

ISRO to Launch ESA's PROBA-3 Mission: A Leap Forward in Space Technology

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is set to launch the European Space Agency’s (ESA) groundbreaking PROBA-3 mission in November 2024, marking a significant milestone in international space collaboration. PROBA-3, the first ESA mission dedicated to precision formation flying, consists of two satellites that will operate as a coordinated unit, simulating a large structure in space. This innovative initiative aims to create an artificial eclipse, allowing scientists to observe the Sun’s faint outer atmosphere, or corona, with unprecedented clarity. By blocking out sunlight, the mission will facilitate in-depth studies of solar phenomena, such as solar flares and coronal mass ejections, which can significantly impact space weather and terrestrial technologies.

ISRO Microsats Flagged Off for Launch Following Assembly at Ananth Technologies

ISRO is set to launch two Space Docking Satellites (SPADEX) aboard PSLV-C60 in December. This experiment aims to enhance India's capabilities in space docking, a crucial step toward future missions, including landing Indian astronauts on the Moon and developing the Bharatiya Antariksha Station (BAS). Additionally, the Microsat-2A and 2B spacecraft have been officially flagged off after completing end-to-end assembly, integration, and testing at Ananth Technologies Pvt. Ltd. in Bengaluru. The ATL team, under the guidance of the U R Rao Satellite Centre (URSC), played a key role in this process.

 Viasat Secures $33.6 Million U.S. Air Force Contract for Advanced Satellite Communications Systems

SIA-India member Viasat, Inc., a leading global satellite communications company, has been awarded a $33.6 million contract by the U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) under the Defense Experimentation Using Commercial Space Internet (DEUCSI) program. The contract, which includes options, focuses on the development and delivery of Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA) systems. These systems aim to provide resilient satellite communications for tactical aircraft, including rotary wing platforms, further enhancing defense capabilities.
