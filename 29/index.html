India Prepares for ISO/TC-20/SC-14 Plenary Meeting dedicated to Space Systems Operations

India is gearing up for active participation in the forthcoming 33rd plenary meeting of ISO/TC-20/SC-14, dedicated to Space Systems and Operations, scheduled to convene from May 13 to May 17, 2024, in Berlin, Germany. The event offers an invaluable platform for industry representatives to engage in international standardization efforts. Concurrently, Working Groups 1 through 8 will conduct meetings, facilitat- ing collaborative discussions and knowledge exchange. This collective endeavor seeks to ensure comprehensive representation of India’s interests at the global level and glean insights into international standardization practices.
In line with India’s commitment to advancing its space capabilities, IN-SPACe is initiating discussions on the adoption of three critical ISO standards pertinent to the Indian space sector:
1. ISO 14303: Space systems — Launch-vehicle-to-spacecraft interfaces
2. ISO 15864: Space systems — General test methods for spacecraft, subsystems, and units
3. ISO 24917: Space systems — General test requirements for launch vehicles
The adoption of these standards underscores India’s dedication to aligning with global best practices and fostering interoperability within the international space community. SIA-India requests you to submit your nominations to be a part of this delegation.

INASA’s Firefly Blue Ghost Mission 1 to Introduce Electric Moon Dust Shield Technology
NASA’s Firefly Blue Ghost Mission 1 is set to introduce a game-changing technology: the Electrodynamic Dust Shield (EDS). Scheduled for de- ployment on the private mission, EDS employs transparent electrodes and electric fields to combat lunar dust, crucial for preserving equipment like solar panels and spacesuits. By mitigating dust-related issues, EDS enhances mission efficiency and safety, aligning with NASA’s broader objectives for sustainable lunar exploration. The success of EDS aboard Firefly Blue Ghost Mission 1 marks a significant leap forward in lunar exploration technology, poised to redefine our understanding of lunar surface operations.

Skyroot Aerospace Set to Elevate India’s Space Ambitions with Vikram-1 Rocket Launch
Skyroot Aerospace, a leading Indian startup, gears up for a landmark moment with the imminent launch of the Vikram-1 rocket. Fol- lowing their trailblazing debut in 2022, the company continues to propel India’s space sector forward, aiming to commercialize space endeavors and cement India’s status as a global space player. The seven-story Vikram-1 rocket represents a significant stride, poised to deploy India’s first privately launched satellites into orbit. Recognized for their pioneering spirit, Skyroot Aerospace’s achievement underscores India’s growing prowess in space technology, marking a pivotal milestone in the nation’s quest for cosmic exploration and innovation.
